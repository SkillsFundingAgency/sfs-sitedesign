East Reading College

March 2017

Dear Colleague,

I am writing to provide your allocation statement for the 2017 to 2018 funding year: it includes the allocations that are relevant to you. Please read it with the following documents: skills funding letter 2017 to 2018 and our funding and performance management rules for the 2017 to 2018 year.

Apprenticeships

Reforms to the apprenticeship system mean that providers will be able to earn apprenticeship funding through different routes. As a result of this, please note that your allocation statement does not include apprenticeship funding at this time. The following section of this letter sets out the reasons for this in more detail. New starts (starts from 1 May 2017)

You will earn funding for new apprenticeship starts through your delivery to levy paying employers and/or non-levy paying employers. Please note that any funding you secure for non-levy starts from 1 May is not included in your allocation statement as it is being communicated separately.

Carry-in learners (apprentices that start before 1 May 2017) We want to ensure that apprentices that start before 1 May 2017 (carry-in learners) are funded until the end of their programmes. We will allocate funding in future years (2017 to 2018 and beyond) to meet the cost of these apprentices.

In early February 2017, we published updated apprenticeship performance management rules for the 2016 to 2017 funding year . These rules set out our commitment to fund carry-in learners ‘reflecting funding previously paid by the Skills Funding Agency’. The next section of the letter explains how we will work out your carry-in allocation.

Adult education budget

Where applicable, funding will be allocated beyond 2017 to 2018 to ensure apprentices that started before 1 May 2017 can complete their programmes.

all apprentices achieve on their planned end date and draw down their achievement payment on this date Allocations for carry-in learners for 2017 to 2018

We will work out the value of your 2017 to 2018 carry-in allocation in June, based on your R10 data return. Calculating your carry-in allocation in June will enable us to work your allocation out on the basis of your actual delivery, rather than us trying to forecast your activity to issue your allocation earlier. We have consulted on this with the Association of Colleges, Association of Employment and Learning Providers and our Provider Reference Group.

We encourage you to submit full and timely data at R10 as this will improve the accuracy of the carry-in allocation we work out for you. We will confirm your carry-in allocation for the 2017 to 2018 funding year in July 2017. The methodology we will use to work out your carry-in will be based on your actual data at R10 and the following assumptions: there is no drop-out

for Trailblazer Standards incentive payments will be drawn down on the date they are due an allowance for data lag.

We continue to allocate your adult education budget as a block grant. We have set the baseline for your 2017 to 2018 allocation at the level of your 2016 to 2017 contract value as at January 2017. It includes the value of any traineeship growth you were awarded at performance management point 1 of the 2016 to 2017 funding year. Where applicable to you, it also includes community learning funding. Your allocation statement does not include any funds you may be awarded through the adult education budget procurement that closed on 27 February. If you submitted a bid, we will notify you of the outcome in May. Funding awarded through the procurement is for the 2017 to 2018 funding year and will not automatically be consolidated in future baselines.

all apprentices remain on programme until their planned end date and draw down all on-programme payments due. Note that for Trailblazer Standards we will first convert any remaining balance to be paid into on-programme payments

Please note that if you only deliver apprenticeships, for the reasons given above, you will not have an allocation statement attached to this letter.

Continuing learners Future devolution of the adult education budget We continue to work with ministers and other stakeholders on the proposals to devolve adult education budget funding to areas with agreed devolution deals. Your 2017 to 2018 adult education budget allocation is not affected by devolution plans.

If you wish to discuss your allocation statement, please contact your provider manager.
Yours sincerely,

Keith Smith
Director – Funding and Programmes
