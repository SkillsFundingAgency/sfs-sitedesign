View national funding formula allocations for schools: 2021 to 2022

Find out how much funding a school would receive if the national funding formula was followed. The numbers presented are based on pupil numbers that are 2 years old.

The government uses the national funding formula to decide how much funding to allocate for mainstream, state-funded schools in England.

This service does not tell you what funding a school will actually receive. Final funding allocations are decided by local authorities.

You can view national funding formula allocations by:

  • individual school
  • multi-academy trust
  • local authority summary

The funding allocations are for 5 to 16 year olds.
